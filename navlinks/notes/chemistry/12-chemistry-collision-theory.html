What is Collision theory? What is Activated Complex theory? What is Thresold Energy? What is Activation Energy?

 

Collision Theory

It attemps to explain how reaction takes place.

The collision theory states that for a reaction to take place, there must be effective collision between reacting molecules.
For collision to be effective:

  • There must be sufficient energy.

  • The two molecular should collide with proper orientation.

  •  

Activated complex theory

According to this theory reactant molecules collide with each other to form activated complex or transition state at first which undergoes decomposition to form product.

 

Theresold Energy (ET)

The energy that transition state of reaction possess that must be achived by the collision for reaction to take place.

Activation Energy (Ea)

The energy that must be supplied to the reactant molecules to make them process thresold energy for reaction to take place is known as activaiton energy.

ie. Activation Energy (Ea) = Theresold Energy (ET) - Reactant energy (Er)

Factors affecting rate of reaction:

Concentration: With increase in concentration the number of effective collisions having energy higher than activation energy.
