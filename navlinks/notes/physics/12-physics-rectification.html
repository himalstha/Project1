Rectification and rectifier

Rectification is the process of conversion of AC to DC. The device that is use in rectification is known as rectifier.

There are two types of rectifiers: 

  1. 1.Half wave rectifier
  2. 2.Full wave rectifier

In Full wave rectifier there are two types of Full wave rectifier:

  1. 1. Center tapped Full wave rectifier
  2. 2. Bridge rectifier

Center Tapped full wave retifier:

The input AC is appied from the primary transformer, Secondary transformer is conneted to the  anode of diode d1 and d2 respectively. The center tapped is connected to the cathod of diode d1 and d2 followed by load resistor.

During positive half cycle, one end of secondary transformer will be positive and one will be negative similarlly diode d1 and d2 will also be one forward biases and reverse biased.

Similary, during negative half cycle, another end of the secondary transformer becomes positive and diode also change from reverse to forward biased and viceversa. 

In both cases, unidirectional curent flows through R Resistor. Thus the combination of d1 and d2 works as full wave rectifier.

Bridge Rectifier:

In Bridge Rectifier Input AC is access from Primary transformer. In Secondary transformer it is connected with diodes that makes a structure like wheatstone bridge in two side. Another two side of that bridge is conneted to the load resistance R.

During Positive half cycle, two diode will be forward biased and two will be backward biased. i.e current flow through only two forward biased diode.

During negative half cycle, another two will be forward biased and viceversa. 

In both case, Unidirectional current flow through resistance R. In this way circuit act as a full wave rectifier.
