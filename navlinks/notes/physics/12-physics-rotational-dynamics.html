Short Notes

Rigid body is defined as a solid body in which the particles are compactly arranged so that the inter-particle distance is small and fixed, and their positions are not disturbed by any external forces applied on it.

Moment of inertia is the sum of the product of the masses of the various particles and square of their perpendicular distances from the axis of rotation.
Moment of inertia is a scalar quantity.
Moment of inertia is also called rotational inertia.
Theorem of parallel axis: The moment of inertia of a body about any axis is equal to its moment of inertia about a parallel axis through its centre of mass plus the product of the mass of the body and the square of the distance between the two axes.
Radius of Gyration: It is defined as the distance from the axis of rotation to a point where the total mass of the body is supposed to be concentrated.
Radius of gyration measures the distribution of mass of a body related to the axis of rotation.
The expression for radius of gyration indicates that it is the root mean square (rms) distance of the particles of the body from the axis of rotation.
In fact, the moment of inertia of any object could be expressed in the form, I = MK^2.
Torque is the turning effect of a force in a body.

Principle of conservation of angular momentum: If no external torque acts on a system, the total angular momentum of the system remains conserved
Couple: Two equal and opposite parallel forces acting on a rigid body at different points constitute a couple

Angular momentum is the moment of linear momentum of an object. Angular momentum is a axial vector.
A dancer on ice spin faster when she folds her arms because of decreasing M.I.
If the earth contracts then the length of the day will decrease.
A boiled egg spins much faster than a raw egg.
A couple produces purely rotational motion.
If a ball rolls then the fraction of the total energy associated with the rotational is 2/7.
A sphere cannot roll on a smooth inclined surface.
