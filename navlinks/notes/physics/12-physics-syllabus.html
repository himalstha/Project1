1. Syllabus of Physics Grade 12.
Notes 12-Physics-syllabus
  1. 1. Rotational dynamics [7 Teaching hours]
  2. 1.1 Equation of angular motion, Relation between linear and angular kinematics
  3. 1.2 Kinetic energy of rotation of rigid body
  4. 1.3 Moment of inertia; Radius of gyration
  5. 1.4 Moment of inertia of a uniform rod
  6. 1.5 Torque and angular acceleration for a rigid body
  7. 1.6 Work and power in rotational motion
  8. 1.7 Angular momentum, conservation of angular momentum.
  1. 2. Periodic motion [6 Teaching hours]
  2. 2.1 Equation of simple harmonic motion (SHM)
  3. 2.2 Energy in SHM
  4. 2.3 Application of SHM: vertical oscillation of mass suspended from coiled spring
  5. 2.4 Angular SHM, simple pendulum
  6. 2.5 Oscillatory motion: Damped oscillation, Forced oscillation and resonance.
  1. 3. Fluid statics [9 Teaching hours]
  2. 3.1 Fluid statics: Pressure in a fluid; Buoyancy
  3. 3.2 Surface tension: Theory of surface tension; Surface energy
  4. 3.3 Angle of contact, capillarity and its applications
  5. 3.4 Fluid Dynamics: Newton’s formula for viscosity in a liquid; Coefficient of viscosity
  6. 3.5 Poiseuille’s formula and its application
  7. 3.6 Stokes law and its applications
  8. 3.7 Equation of continuity and its applications
  9. 3.8 Bernoulli’s equation and its applications.
  1. 4. First Law of Thermodynamics [6 Teaching hours]
  2. 4.1 Thermodynamic systems
  3. 4.2 Thermodynamic processes: Adiabatic, isochoric, isothermal and isobaric
  4. 4.3 Heat capacities of an ideal gas at constant pressure and volume and relation between them
  5. 4.4 Isothermal and Adiabatic processes for an ideal gas.
  1. 5. Second Law of Thermodynamics [6 Teaching hours]
  2. 5.1 Thermodynamic systems and direction of thermodynamic processes
  3. 5.2 Second law of thermodynamics
  4. 5.3 Heat engines
  5. 5.4 Internal combustion engines: Otto cycle, Diesel cycle; Carnot cycle
  6. 5.5 Refrigerator
  7. 5.6 Entropy and disorder (introduction only)
  1. Content Area: Wave and Optics
  2. 6. Wave motion [2 Teaching hours]
  3. 6.1 Progressive waves
  4. 6.2 Mathematical description of a wave
  5. 6.3 Stationary waves
  1. 7. Mechanical waves [4 Teaching hours]
  2. 7.1 Speed of wave motion; Velocity of sound in solid and liquid
  3. 7.2 Velocity of sound in gas
  4. 7.4 Effect of temperature, pressure, humidity on velocity of sound.
  1. 8. Wave in pipes and strings [4 Teaching hours]
  2. 8.1 Stationary waves in closed and open pipes
  3. 8.2 Harmonics and overtones in closed and open organ pipes
  4. 8.3 End correction in pipes
  5. 8.4 Velocity of transverse waves along a stretched string
  6. 8.5 Vibration of string and overtones
  7. 8.6 Laws of vibration of fixed string.
  1. 9. Acoustic phenomena [5 Teaching hours]
  2. 9.1 Sound waves: Pressure amplitude
  3. 9.2 Characteristics of sound: Intensity; loudness, quality and pitch
  4. 9.3 Doppler’s effect.
  1. 10. Nature and propagation of Light [3 Teaching hours]
  2. 10.1 Huygen’s principle
  3. 10.2 Reflection and Refraction according to wave theory
  1. 11. Interference [2 Teaching hours]
  2. 11.1 Phenomenon of Interferences: Coherent sources
  3. 11.2 Young’s double slit experiment.
  1. 12. Diffraction [3 Teaching hours]
  2. 12.1 Diffraction from a single slit
  3. 12.2 Diffraction pattern of image; Diffraction grating
  4. 12.3 Resolving power of optical instruments.
  1. 13. Polarization [3 Teaching hours]
  2. 13.1 Phenomenon of polarization
  3. 13.2 Brewster’s law; transverse nature of light
  4. 13.3 Polaroid.
  1. Content Area: Electricity and Magnetism
  2. 14.Electrical circuits [6 Teaching hours]
  3. 14.1 Kirchhoff’s law
  4. 14.2 Wheatstone bridge circuit; Meter bridge
  5. 14.3 Potentiometer: Comparison of e.m.f., measurement of internal resistances of a cell
  6. 14.4 Super conductors; Perfect conductors
  7. 14.5 Conversion of galvanometer into voltmeter and ammeter; Ohmmeter
  8. 14.6 Joule’s law
  1. 15. Thermoelectric effects: [3 Teaching hours]
  2. 15.1 Seebeck effect; Thermocouples
  3. 15.2 Peltier effect: Variation of thermoelectric e.m.f. with temperature; Thermopile
  1. 16. Magnetic field [9 Teaching hours]
  2. 16.1 Magnetic field lines and magnetic flux; Oersted’s experiment
  3. 16.2 Force on moving charge; Force on a conductor
  4. 16.3 Force and Torque on rectangular coil, Moving coil galvanometer
  5. 16.4 Hall effect
  6. 16.5 Magnetic field of a moving charge
  7. 16.6 Biot and Savart law and its application to
    1. (i) a circular coil
    2. (ii) a long straight conductor
    3. (iii) a long solenoid
  8. 16.7 Ampere’s law and its applications to
    1. (i) a long straight conductor
    2. (ii) a straight solenoid
    3. (iii) a toroidal solenoid
  9. 16.8 Force between two parallel conductors carrying current- definition of ampere
  1. 17. Magnetic properties of materials: [5 Teaching hours]
  2. 17.1 Magnetic field lines and magnetic flux
  3. 17.2 Flux density in magnetic material; Relative permeability; Susceptibility
  1. 18. Electromagnetic Induction: [6 Teaching hours]
  2. 18.1 Faraday’s laws; Induced electric fields
  3. 18.2 Lenz’s law, Motional electromotive force
  4. 18.3 A.C. generators; Eddy currents
  5. 18.4 Self-inductance and mutual inductance
  6. 18.5 Energy stored in an inductor
  7. 18.6 Transformer.
  1. 19. Alternating Currents [6 Teaching hours]
  2. 19.1 Peak and rms value of AC current and voltage
  3. 19.2 AC through a resistor, a capacitor and an inductor
  4. 19.3 Phasor diagram
  5. 19.4 Series circuits containing combination of resistance, capacitance and inductance
  6. 19.5 Series resonance, quality factor
  7. 19.6 Power in AC circuits: power factor

  4. 20.2 Motion of electron beam in electric and magnetic fields

  4. 22.3 Rectification and rectifier
  5. 22.4 Logic gates; NOT, OR, AND, NAND and NOR.

  2. 23.1 Bohr’s theory of hydrogen atom
  3. 23.2 Spectral series; Excitation and ionization potentials
  4. 23.3 Energy level; Emission and absorption spectra
  5. 23.4 De Broglie Theory; Duality
  6. 23.5 Uncertainly principle
  7. 23.6 X-rays: Nature and production; uses
  8. 23.7 X-rays diffraction, Bragg’s law.

  2. 24.1 Alpha-particles; Beta-particles, Gamma rays

  4. 24.3 Half-life, mean-life and decay constant

  6. 24.5 Carbon dating
  7. 24.6 Medical use of nuclear radiation and possible health hazard.
