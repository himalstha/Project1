Thermoelectric effects:

Content:

  • Seebeck effect; Thermocouples

Thermoelectric effect The phenomenon of producing electricity by keeping the junction of thermocouple at different temperature is called thermoelectric effect. Thus produced current is called thermoelectric current and the emf is called thermoelectric emf. The thermo emf depends upon the nature of material forming the thermocouple and difference in temperature between the junction.

Thermo-couple: A pair of dissimilar metal forming a couple in order to produce electricity is called thermocouple.

Thermoelectric series: An arrangement of metal forming a thermocouple in order to produce thermoelectricity is called thermoelectric series. The thermoelectric series is given below

Sb, Fe, Zn, Ag, Pb, Cu, Pt, Co, Bi

From thermoelectric series we can say that greater the length between the metal selected from thermoelectric series more will be the emf produced and vice versa. Also the direction of current is from earlier metal to later one in thermoelectric series through cold junction.

 

Seebeck effect:

The phenomenon of producing thermoelectric current by keeping the junction of thermocouple at different temperature is called seebeck effect. It is also known as thermoelectric effect

Peltier effect: The phenomenon of evolution or absorption heat across the junction of thermocouple when electric current passes through it is called Peltier effect. It is inverse process of seebeck effect.

Thomson effect: The phenomenon of evolution or absorption of heat when electric current passes through unequally heated conductor is called Thomson effect.

Positive Thomson effect: If heat is evolved across the junction when electric current passes through hotter side to colder side of a conductor then this type of effect is called positive Thomson effect. For example copper, silver, zinc, antimony, cadmium etc.

Negative Thomson effect : If heat is absorbed across the junction when electric current passes through hotter side to colder side of a conductor then this type of effect is called negative Thomson effect. For example iron, bismuth, platinum, cobalt etc.

Neutral Thomson effect: Neither heat is evolved nor absorbed across the junction when electric current passes through hotter side to colder side or vice versa is called neutral Thomson effect. For example lead ğ‘ƒğ‘ . So lead is used as standard metal in thermoelectricity.
