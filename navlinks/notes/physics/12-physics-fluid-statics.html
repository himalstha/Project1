Fluid Statics:
Fluid statics is the study of fluids at rest, and it deals with the pressure and forces in a fluid.

Pressure in a fluid:
Pressure in a fluid is the force per unit area exerted on a surface by the fluid. The pressure in a fluid decreases with increasing depth due to the weight of the fluid above it.

Buoyancy:
Buoyancy is the upward force exerted on an object immersed in a fluid, due to the difference in pressure between the top and bottom of the object. Archimedes' principle states that the buoyant force is equal to the weight of the fluid displaced by the object.

Surface Tension:
Surface tension is the property of a liquid that causes its surface to be slightly contracted and behave as though it were a thin sheet. The theory of surface tension states that the molecules at the surface of a liquid are attracted to each other more strongly than they are to the molecules in the air.

Surface Energy:
Surface energy is the energy associated with the formation of new surfaces in a material. It is a measure of the energy required to create a new surface and is proportional to the surface area.

 

Angle of Contact:
The angle of contact is the angle between a liquid surface and a solid surface in contact with it. It is used to determine the shape of a liquid in a container, as well as its height and volume.

Capillarity:
Capillarity is the ability of a liquid to rise or fall in a narrow tube due to the intermolecular forces between the liquid and the solid surface of the tube. This phenomenon is used in many applications, including ink pens and blood pressure monitoring.

 

Fluid Dynamics:
Fluid dynamics is the study of fluids in motion, and it deals with the velocity and pressure of fluids.

Newton's Formula for Viscosity in a Liquid:

Newton's formula for viscosity in a liquid states that the shear stress in a fluid is proportional to the velocity gradient in the fluid. It is expressed as:

τ = η * dv/dy

where τ is the shear stress, η is the viscosity, and dv/dy is the velocity gradient.

 

Coefficient of Viscosity:

The coefficient of viscosity is a measure of a fluid's resistance to flow. It is defined as the ratio of shear stress to the velocity gradient in the fluid.

 

Poiseuille's Formula:

Poiseuille's formula is used to calculate the pressure drop in a fluid flowing through a pipe or a vessel. It is expressed as:

ΔP = (8 * η * L * Q) / (π * r^4)

where ΔP is the pressure drop, η is the viscosity, L is the length of the pipe, Q is the flow rate, and r is the radius of the pipe.

 

 

Stokes Law:

Stokes law is used to calculate the force required to move a small spherical object through a fluid. It is expressed as:

F = 6 * π * η * r * v

 where F is the force, η is the viscosity, r is the radius of the sphere, and v is the velocity of the sphere.

 

Equation of Continuity:

The equation of continuity states that the rate of flow of a fluid through a pipe or channel must remain constant. It is expressed as:

A1 * v1 = A2 * v2

where A1 and A2 are the cross-sectional areas of the pipe or channel, and v1 and v2 are the velocities of the fluid at the two points.

 

Bernoulli's Equation:

Bernoulli's equation is used to describe the relationship between pressure and velocity in a fluid. It states that the total energy of a fluid, including pressure and velocity energy, remains constant along a streamline. It is expressed as:

P + 1/2 * ρ * v^2 + ρ * g * h = constant

where P is the pressure, ρ is the density of the fluid, v is the velocity of the fluid, g is the acceleration due to gravity, and h is the height of the fluid.

 

Bernoulli's equation is widely used in many applications, such as fluid flow in pipes, fluid flow in open channels, fluid flow in venturis, fluid flow in orifices, fluid flow in nozzle and many more. It helps in understanding the pressure distribution in a fluid and how it affects the flow of the fluid.
