Mechanical waves

Mechanical waves are waves that require a medium for their propagation and transfer energy through the medium. They include sound waves, seismic waves, and ocean waves.

 

The speed of wave motion is dependent on the properties of the medium, such as its density and elasticity.

The velocity of sound in solids is higher than in liquids, which is higher than in gases. This is because solids have a higher density and elasticity compared to liquids and gases, allowing sound waves to travel faster.

 

The velocity of sound in a gas is dependent on the temperature, pressure, and humidity of the gas. At a constant temperature, the velocity of sound in a gas increases with pressure and decreases with humidity. An increase in temperature also leads to an increase in the velocity of sound.

 

Effect of temperature, pressure, humidity on velocity of sound.

These effects can be explained by the relationship between the velocity of sound and the elastic and thermal properties of the medium. A higher temperature and pressure result in a more compressible and elastic medium, allowing sound waves to travel faster. Humidity decreases the velocity of sound by reducing the density of the air, making it a less efficient medium for sound waves to travel through.
