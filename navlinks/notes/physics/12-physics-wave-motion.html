Wave motion

Wave motion refers to the transfer of energy through a medium without any permanent transfer of matter.

There are two types of wave motion: progressive and stationary.

 

Progressive waves

Progressive waves, also known as traveling waves, move through a medium and transport energy from one point to another. Examples include ocean waves and sound waves.

 

The mathematical description of a wave:

The mathematical description of a wave is given by the wave equation, which relates the wave's velocity, frequency, and wavelength. The wave equation can be used to calculate the behavior of waves in different media, such as air, water, and solids.

 

Stationary waves:

Stationary waves, also known as standing waves, occur when two waves of equal frequency and amplitude interfere with each other, creating a wave pattern that does not move. Stationary waves can be observed in strings, air columns, and other vibrating systems. The standing wave pattern is characterized by nodes and antinodes, where the amplitude of the wave is either at a minimum or maximum, respectively.
