Notes 12-Physics-syllabus Unit and Dimension
Quantities having the same Dimensional Formula
o Impulse and momentum.
o Work, energy, torque, the moment of force, energy.
o Angular momentum, Planck's constant, rotational impulse.
o Stress, pressure, modulus of elasticity, energy density.
o Force constant, surface tension, surface energy.
o Angular velocity, frequency, velocity gradient.
o Gravitational potential, latent heat.
o Thermal capacity, entropy, universal gas constant and Boltzmann's constant.
o Force, thrust.
o Power, luminous flux.
Dimensionless quantities:
Dimensionless quantities are those which do not have dimensions but have a fixed value.
o Dimensionless quantities without units: Pure numbers, л, e, sin e, cos e, tan e etc.
o Dimensionless quantities with units: Angular displacement - radian, Joule's constant - joule/calorie, etc.
Dimensionless variables:
Dimensionless variables are those physical quantities which do not have dimensions and do not have a fixed value. For example Specific gravity, refractive index, the coefficient of friction, Poisson's ratio, etc.

Keep in Mind:
Angstrom is the unit of length used to measure the wavelength of light. 1 Å = 10-10 m.
Fermi is the unit of length used to measure nuclear distances. 1 Fermi = 10-15 meter.
A light year is the unit of length for measuring astronomical distances.
Light year = distance traveled by light in 1 year = 9.4605 x 1015 m.
Astronomical unit = Mean distance between the sun and earth = 1.5 x 1011 m.
Parsec = 3.26 light years = 3.084x1016 m
Barn is the unit of area for measuring scattering cross-section of collisions. 1 barn = 10-28 m2.
Chronometer and metronome are time measuring instruments. The quantity having the same unit in all the systems of units is time.
